🌍 Gravity
The invisible force that pulls everything down
🎯 Big Idea
The direction of "down" is always toward the center of the Earth. Gravity pulls everything toward the Earth's core!

Why do astronauts float on the Moon?
👆 Tap to reveal

🍎
Why does a dropped apple always fall DOWN?
👆 Tap to reveal

🔥 Friction
The force that slows things down
🎯 Big Idea
Friction happens when two surfaces rub together. It slows moving objects down. The rougher the surfaces, the more friction!
🌊 Mystery Question
❓ Why do you move SO fast on a water slide?
💧
Water REDUCES Friction!
Water acts as a lubricant between you and the slide surface. Less friction = less resistance = you zoom down super fast! 🚀

On a dry slide, friction between your skin and the slide would slow you down.

💡 Magnetism
Magnetism is the invisible force created by magnets. It can work through some materials — like paper, plastic, and air — without even touching!

Q1 • Forces
What is the standard unit used to measure force?
👆 Tap for answer
⚖️ The Newton (N) — named after scientist Sir Isaac Newton!
Q2 • Gravity
In a vacuum, which falls faster: a heavy rock or a light leaf?
👆 Tap for answer
🌍 Neither! They fall at exactly the same speed. Gravity pulls all objects equally!
Q3 • Friction
What happens to friction when two rough surfaces rub together?
👆 Tap for answer
🔥 Friction increases! Rough surfaces have more tiny bumps that grip each other, creating more resistance!
Q4 • Magnets
What happens when you put two North poles of magnets together?
👆 Tap for answer
❌ They REPEL each other! Same poles always push away. Only opposite poles (N+S) attract!
Q5 • Materials
Name TWO materials that are magnetic and TWO that are NOT.
👆 Tap for answer
🧲 Magnetic: Iron, Steel, Nickel, Cobalt
❌ Not magnetic: Plastic, Wood, Glass, Copper, Aluminium
Q6 • Friction
What tool do we use to measure the strength of a force?
👆 Tap for answer
📏 A Forcemeter (also called a Newton Meter)! It stretches a spring inside to measure force in Newtons (N).
